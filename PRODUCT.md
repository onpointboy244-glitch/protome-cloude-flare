# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential customers and visitors discovering protome — a new product or service. They come with curiosity, seeking clarity on what protome offers and whether it fits their needs. The site should earn their attention, not demand it.

## Product Purpose

A landing site that markets protome to its audience. The purpose is to communicate what protome is, why it matters, and what makes it distinct — in a way that feels premium, confident, and trustworthy rather than noisy or transactional.

## Brand Personality

Premium, sophisticated, confident. The voice is restrained but not cold — it lets quality speak through craft rather than volume. Every element earns its place.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS look**: No gradient heroes, no mockup screenshots inset into laptops, no big-number-stat rows, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section. No templated startup marketing.
- **Noisy or crowded layouts**: Nothing that feels like it's trying too hard to sell.
- **Corporate stiffness**: Nothing bureaucratic, formal, or generic-corporate.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through restraint** — The fewer elements on the page, the more each one matters. Every component serves a purpose; nothing is decorative filler.
2. **Craft as the differentiator** — Typography, spacing, motion, and color quality do the selling, not loud assertions. The polish of the execution is the message.
3. **Earn attention, don't demand it** — The page draws the user in through intrigue and quality, not through urgency or pressure. Respect the visitor's time and intelligence.
4. **Anti-template** — Nothing about protome's landing page should look like it came from a startup template. Avoid the familiar patterns of modern SaaS marketing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard web best practices. Target WCAG AA. Reduced motion support for animations. Color contrast of ≥4.5:1 for body text.
